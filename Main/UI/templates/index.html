Pneumonia is a respiratory infection that affects the lungs, causing inflammation and filling them with fluid or pus. It can be caused by a variety of microorganisms, such as bacteria, viruses, or fungi. The symptoms of pneumonia can range from mild to severe and can include cough, fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue. Pneumonia can be particularly dangerous for young children, the elderly, and individuals with weakened immune systems. Treatment for pneumonia typically involves antibiotics, antiviral medications, or antifungal drugs depending on the underlying cause. In severe cases, hospitalization may be necessary to provide oxygen and respiratory support. To prevent pneumonia, individuals can practice good hygiene, such as washing their hands frequently, avoiding close contact with people who are sick, and getting vaccinated against bacterial and viral infections that can lead to pneumonia.
One of the key challenges in managing pneumonia is early diagnosis, as symptoms can be similar to other respiratory illnesses. This project aims to address this challenge by providing a tool for early detection of pneumonia through the analysis of chest X-rays. By using machine learning algorithms, the project can quickly and accurately identify X-rays that show signs of pneumonia, allowing for timely treatment and ultimately improving patient outcomes
